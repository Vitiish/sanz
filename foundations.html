FIRST YEAR: FUNDAMENTALS OF ENGINEERING



UNIT 1 - Background and Historical Perspectives

SUMMARY:
This unit talks about the history and background of computing, from the first generations to today. On the other hand, it deals with very significant characters in the computer world such as one of the fathers of computer science, Alan Turing, who invented the Enigma machine to decipher the Nazi code. There is also talk of various computer languages such as Python, BASIC, COBOL, C or HTML among others.



UNIT 2 - The current context of Computer Science

SUMMARY:
In this unit it is about knowing what is the meaning of Computer Engineering, what is an Engineer or how to become a good Engineer.



UNIT 3 - Computer Engineering as a Degree and Profession

SUMMARY:
This unit deals with how Computer Engineers function in the real world based on the branch they are dedicated to, it also gives an overview and what qualities they must have to be engineers. Some branches can be, for example, the branch of Cybersecurity, Electronics, Hardware design...



UNIT 4 - Hardware

SUMMARY:
This unit deals with Hardware components such as what is a motherboard, RAM memory, graphics card... On the other hand, it also studies the different types of servers, what is computational architecture or the processes that are given to computers work.



UNIT 5 - Software

SUMMARY:
This unit deals with the different levels and systems that a computer has for its proper functioning, such as the different types of software (Application Software, Utility Software...) or the different phases of development (Kavan, Rad, Waterfall...).



UNIT 6 - Operating System

SUMMARY:
This unit covers concepts such as what is a user, what is a daemon, and many more. At the same time, it also deals with the different Processes of Memory Administration or Input/Output operations in devices... among others.



UNIT 7 - Networks

SUMMARY:
This unit deals with networks in general. It shows the basic concepts of what is a network?, its different types, the different "versions" of the World Wide Web and the origin of the Arpanet.



UNIT 8 - New Trends I and II

SUMMARY:
This unit deals with the different trends over the years and how we have advanced thanks to the development of Artificial Intelligence, robots... It also explains concepts such as what is a ledger referring to the subject of Blockchain and Bitcoin for example.



UNIT 9 - Information and Data

SUMMARY:
This unit deals with the different conversion units such as, for example, the binary, hexadecimal, octal system..., what is a mantissa or how to do the a2 complement. Also, it talks about what a bit and a byte are or other concepts like what RGB is.



UNIT 10 - Data Foundations

SUMMARY:
This unit deals with the different foundations of the Data, the types of structures (structured, semistructured and unestructured), the different types of databases (SQL, NoSQL...) and the relationships established between them so that they work correctly.
